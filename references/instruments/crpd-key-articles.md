# CRPD: Rapid Operational Reference

This file provides a rapid operational reference to key articles of the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD).

Use this file for analytical triage to identify core CRPD articles, minimum standards, typical domestic legal red flags, and main interpretive materials. For deeper substantive reference, consult `crpd.md`.

## Article 5: Equality and non-discrimination
**Minimum Standard:** Prohibition of all discrimination on the basis of disability; guarantee of equal and effective legal protection against discrimination on all grounds; provision of reasonable accommodation.
**Red Flags:** Domestic legal instruments that do not explicitly prohibit discrimination based on disability; failure to define the denial of reasonable accommodation as a form of discrimination.
**Interpretive Material:** CRPD Committee General Comment No. 6 (2018).

## Article 9: Accessibility
**Minimum Standard:** Identification and elimination of obstacles and barriers to accessibility in the physical environment, transportation, information and communications, and other facilities and services open or provided to the public.
**Red Flags:** Lack of enforceable accessibility standards; absence of sanctions for non-compliance; reliance on voluntary guidelines rather than mandatory regulations.
**Interpretive Material:** CRPD Committee General Comment No. 2 (2014).

## Article 12: Equal recognition before the law
**Minimum Standard:** Recognition of legal capacity on an equal basis with others in all aspects of life; provision of access to the support persons with disabilities may require in exercising their legal capacity.
**Red Flags:** Existence of guardianship, interdiction, or other forms of substituted decision-making; laws restricting the right to own or inherit property, control financial affairs, or have equal access to bank loans, mortgages, and other forms of financial credit based on disability.
**Interpretive Material:** CRPD Committee General Comment No. 1 (2014).

## Article 19: Living independently and being included in the community
**Minimum Standard:** The equal right of all persons with disabilities to live in the community, with choices equal to others; access to a range of in-home, residential, and other community support services, including personal assistance necessary to support living and inclusion in the community, and to prevent isolation or segregation.
**Red Flags:** Domestic laws or policies that promote or fund institutionalisation; lack of community-based support services; legal mechanisms that permit involuntary commitment based on disability.
**Interpretive Material:** CRPD Committee General Comment No. 5 (2017).

## Article 24: Education
**Minimum Standard:** An inclusive education system at all levels and lifelong learning; reasonable accommodation of the individual's requirements; provision of the support required within the general education system.
**Red Flags:** Segregated "special education" systems; legal provisions allowing schools to refuse admission based on disability or the cost of accommodation; lack of training for teachers in inclusive education methods.
**Interpretive Material:** CRPD Committee General Comment No. 4 (2016).

## Article 27: Work and employment
**Minimum Standard:** The right to work, on an equal basis with others, in a labour market and work environment that is open, inclusive, and accessible; prohibition of discrimination on the basis of disability with regard to all matters concerning all forms of employment.
**Red Flags:** Sheltered workshops or employment settings that pay below minimum wage; lack of legal requirements for workplace accommodation; discriminatory hiring practices legally permitted.
**Interpretive Material:** CRPD Committee General Comment No. 8 (2022).
