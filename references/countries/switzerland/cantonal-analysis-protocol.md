# Cantonal Analysis Protocol
## Operational Subnational Logic for Switzerland

This document details the specific operational protocol for assessing normative alignment at the cantonal level within the Swiss federal structure for NormTrace.

It provides a framework for analysing domestic legal instruments enacted by Swiss cantons, ensuring they are interpreted within their unique constitutional and linguistic contexts.

## 1. Canton identification
Identify the specific canton whose legislation is under review. This is crucial as cantons possess significant autonomy and their legal frameworks can vary widely.

## 2. Competence check
Verify whether the subject matter of the domestic legal instrument falls within the competence of the canton or the Confederation.
- Is the issue exclusively federal, concurrent, or exclusively cantonal?
- Does the cantonal law implement federal law or act independently?

## 3. Federal compatibility test
Assess whether the cantonal legal instrument complies with federal law and international treaties binding on Switzerland.
- Does the cantonal law conflict with a federal act or the Federal Constitution?
- Is it compatible with international human rights standards, such as the CRPD?

## 4. Use of cantonal messages / explanatory reports
Consult the official explanatory reports (Messages/Botschaften) accompanying cantonal legislative drafts. These are essential interpretive materials in Switzerland.
- What was the legislative intent behind the provision?
- Does the message reference international standards or federal obligations?

## 5. Multilingual caution
Switzerland has four national languages. Ensure that the analysis accounts for potential translation nuances or differences between the official language versions (German, French, Italian) of federal laws and the language used in the specific cantonal legislation.

## 6. Landsgemeinde note
Be aware of unique legislative processes in certain cantons. For example, in Appenzell Innerrhoden and Glarus, laws are still passed by the Landsgemeinde (cantonal assembly of citizens). This direct democratic mechanism can influence the framing and interpretation of cantonal laws.

## 7. Implementation variability across cantons
Recognise that the implementation of international standards and federal laws can differ significantly across the 26 cantons.
- Avoid generalising findings from one canton to the entire country.
- Acknowledge that a legal provision may be interpreted or applied differently depending on the specific cantonal administrative or judicial context.
